Knowing Argentina

Discovering the world

Argentina is a South American country, located at the edge of the continent. It borders five other countries, namely: Bolivia, Brazil, Chile, Paraguay and Uruguay.

Argentina is the third most populous country in South America and 33rd in the world. Population density is 15 people per square kilometer of land area, well below the world average of 50 people.

Argentina's economy is the third largest in Latin America, with a high quality of life and a high GDP per capita, in addition to being considered an upper-middle income economy.

Localization

Argentina is a South American country, located at far end of the continent, has a vast coastal area with the Atlantic Ocean. It is the second largest country in Latin America and the eighth in the world. Its population is the fourth largest in the Americas. The country is divided into twenty-three provinces and has borders with five countries, namely: Bolivia, Brazil, Chile, Paraguay and Uruguay.

Relief

The predominant reliefs are: plains, plateaus and mountainous elevations. As for biodiversity, the pampas are fertile plains in the center of the country with low vegetation and few trees, Patagonia is a plateau region located to the south, with few trees, undergrowth, bushes, lakes and glaciers, in Cuyo is where the highest peak of the Andes Mountain range is located (almost seven thousand meters).

Vegetation

Argentina's vegetation is very rich and diverse, with an emphasis on the region's fauna, both natural aspects directly determined by the corresponding differences in climate, soil and other material conditions.
In the north of Argentine Mesopotamia, hot and humid, subtropical forests predominate, in which species such as cedar, ipe, mate, pine, long ferns, bamboo and vines are identified. Along the riverbeds, this vegetation extends to the southern part of the Mesopotamian plain.

Fauna

There are lots of birds, reptiles and rodents. Among the mammals are the jaguar, the cougar, the pampas cat. There are foxes in several regions and, in the subtropical forest, the howler monkey is widely found, as well as the weasel, the tapir, the giant anteater, the armadillo, several species of deer and, in the Andean regions, a llama, an alpaca, the vicuna and the guanaco. The rodent order is well represented, with capybaras and various types of rabbit.

Ores

The official name is Republic of Argentina; however, this name is rarely used. Its name comes from Latin, derived from the word "Argentum" that signify silver (this name was chosen because of the legends that affirm that there would be a lot of this valued metal in the region).
Argentina has good mineral resources. In addition to oil and natural gas, it also has iron, aluminum, uranium and zinc. Argentina's main trading partners are Brazil, the United States of America, Italy and the Netherlands.

Climate

Argentina's climate varies from region to region, whose main climatic types are: from hills to the northwest, southeast and west; tropical arid to the south; cold arid to the southwest, temperate continental to the south; tropical to the north; and subpolar in the extreme south.
Much of the Argentine territory is located in the temperate zone of the southern hemisphere. The country has tropical and subtropical, arid and cold climates, with different combinations and contrasts, resulting from variations in altitude and other factors. In almost all regions of Argentina there is occasional snowfall, except in the far north, where a tropical climate prevails. In this same area, the days are hot from October to March, cold and dry from April to September.

Society

Argentines are citizens of Argentina or their descendants abroad. The Argentine nation is a multi-ethnic society, that is, it is a nation of people from different ethnic backgrounds. As a result, the Argentine people do not consider their nationality as an ethnic group, but as a citizenship, made up of several ethnic groups. In addition to the indigenous population, almost all Argentines or their ancestors emigrated in the last five centuries.

Population

According to the 2010 census carried out by the National Institute of Statistics and Censuses of Argentina (INDEC), Argentina had a population of 40,091,359 inhabitants, of which 1,805,957, or 4.6%, were born in other countries. The population growth rate in 2008 was estimated at 0.917% per year, with a birth rate of 16.32 live births per thousand inhabitants and a mortality rate of 7.54 deaths per thousand inhabitants.
Argentina has forty million inhabitants (the third largest on the South American continent), its demographic density is 15 inhab./k². More than ninety percent of people live in urban areas. The society is formed, mainly, by descendants of native peoples (Guarani, for example) and of people coming from Europe, being a country of many ethnicities.

Ancestry

In the colonial period, the Argentine population was formed by the mixing of pre-Columbian indigenous peoples with a colonizing population of Spanish origin and with a few slaves from sub-Saharan Africa. Until the mid-nineteenth century, the ethnic composition of Argentina was very similar to that of other Hispanic American countries, with a large part of the population being made up of mestizo descendants of Spanish men with indigenous women.

Language

After Spanish colonization in Latin America, each country adapted its language by merging that of indigenous peoples with Spanish. If in Spain only the original Spanish is not spoken, let alone in another continent and in the extreme south of the world.
The Spanish spoken in Argentina is called Latin Spanish, which had its origins in Castilian. The term Castilian was given when the Kings of Castile reigned over Spain and the language was nicknamed in honor of them. Over time, the language of Argentina, Uruguay and part of Chile differed in some aspects.

Gastronomy

The cuisine of this country differs from the rest of Latin America due to the great European contributions: Italian and Spanish, which are two very important influences. This explains why meat is one of the main ingredients in this cuisine. Meat is usually accompanied by French fries, salads, provolone or provoleta (smoked provolone).
However, it seems to spread in Argentine restaurants the tendency to divulge traditional dishes rather than the typical dishes of the first inhabitants of the region.

Culture

Argentina's culture is diverse, thanks to the country's pluricultural character, one of its geographic varieties is the combination of the many ethnic identities — mainly European — that make up its population. Among them there is Tango (Hola) as it is called in western regions.
Argentine culture has its origins in a mixture of others that met during the period of immigration. As for ideologies, social democratic thought and language stand out, as well as faith in freedom, democracy and respect for human rights.

Economy

Argentina's economy is the third largest in Latin America,[150] with a high quality of life and a high GDP per capita,[151] and is considered an upper-middle income economy.
The country has natural resources such as natural gas (where it is the largest producer in Latin America) and the extraction of lithium,[153] silver[154] and gold,[155] a highly literate population, an export-oriented agricultural sector and a diversified industrial base. Historically, however, Argentina's economic performance has been very uneven, where high economic growth has alternated with periods of severe recessions, especially during the late 20th century, in addition to problems such as poor income distribution and rising incomes. poverty.

Inflation

Argentina is considered an emerging market by the FTSE Global Equity Index and is one of the G20 economies. However, high inflation rates have been a constant weakness of the country's economy for decades.[158] officially swinging around 9% since 2006, the country's inflation is estimated at over 30% by independent sources,[159] raising criticism that the government has manipulated official inflation statistics.[160] The urban poverty rate has fallen below of the 2001 economic crisis indices.[161] Income distribution has improved since 2002, but is still remarkably unequal.[162][163] Argentina began a period of fiscal austerity in 2012, due to a slowdown process economic.

Economic Crises

The loss of purchasing power of Argentines worsened last year, when the economy plummeted 9.9% as a result of the stoppage of activities decreed by the covid-19 pandemic. Poverty has grown by 6.5 percentage points and now affects 42% of the population, the highest figure since the 2001-2002 corralito crisis.
The readjustments approved in 2021 for the minimum wage, pensions and collective sectorial negotiations (known in Argentina as parity) sought to reverse this situation. However, its success depends on inflation not accelerating, a condition that few believe is possible given the high issuance of currency.

Government

Argentina is a constitutional republic and a representative democracy. The government is regulated by a system of three independent powers defined by Argentina's Constitution, which serves as the country's ultimate legislation. The seat of government is the city of Buenos Aires. Suffrage is universal, egalitarian, secret and mandatory.
Despite being declared the capital in 1853, Buenos Aires did not become the official capital until 1880. There were moves to move the administrative center elsewhere. During Raúl Alfonsín's presidency, a law was passed to transfer the federal capital to Viedma, Río Negro. Studies were underway when economic problems halted the project in 1989. Although the law was never formally repealed, it is now treated as a relic.
